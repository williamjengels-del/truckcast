Polish work praise: Brad is a peer with full input rights and equity-aligned interest, not a hired designer executing to spec. Leading with credit for the craft he's done sets the conversation as collaborative, not critique-from-above.
Cut testimonials entirely: Three-testimonial walls are dated SaaS pattern. One testimonial looks sparse. And the mockup's Maria and James were placeholders, not real operators — fabricated testimonials on a public site are a credibility risk if anyone ever looks them up. The stats row does the credibility work more honestly.
Cut "How it works": Generic SaaS boilerplate every operations tool has. Copy like "Connect your calendar and POS... Take inquiries, log sales... Get smarter forecasts" doesn't convey what makes VendCast different. Every SaaS makes these claims. Filler that doesn't do trust work or product demonstration.
Cut "Predict Your Success" orange callout: Reduces VendCast's most sophisticated feature (weather-adjusted forecasting with confidence ranges, plain-English adjustments, network-enhanced predictions) to generic "AI-powered sales predictions" language. Also fights the teal brand visually and reads as the most ad-feeling element on the page — exactly what Brad objected to.
Remove sparkle emoji (✨): 2019 SaaS marketing signal. Doesn't match VendCast's operator-serious voice. Operators aren't the audience for whimsy — they're running businesses where margins matter.
No emojis anywhere else: Same reasoning. Keep the voice direct and unembellished. Emojis in this context would undercut the confidence the data-driven insight blocks need to carry.
Cut 5-star rating graphics: Feels Yelp/Google-review generic. VendCast should feel like a tool operators rely on, not a service they rate.
Move pricing to its own tab: Already in nav. Homepage doesn't need to sell on price — 14 days free with no credit card is the real hook. Price becomes a question operators ask themselves once they've decided they want VendCast. Keeping it on the homepage creates "trying to sell you" energy Brad's reacting to. Also: Linear, Notion, and other serious SaaS tools do this.
Keep roadmap on its own tab: Already in nav. Duplicating on homepage creates clutter. The roadmap transparency is a differentiator, but it's more valuable as a destination operators navigate to than as a preview tile.
Three insight blocks below hero: The single strongest content VendCast can put on a homepage. Every operator wants sales, events, and bookings — money is the gravity everyone falls toward. Data-backed insights demonstrate catalyst behavior (VendCast making money happen that wouldn't otherwise) without preying, without fabricating testimonials, and without overclaiming outcomes. Five years of real operator data is a content asset no other mobile-vendor SaaS has access to.
Weather block (negative anchor): Universal pain point every mobile vendor recognizes. Operators have all had weather-destroyed events and attributed them to bad luck. Quantifying the pattern shows VendCast reveals something their gut can't. Negative framing hooks attention quickly because it names existing pain.
Repeat bookings block (universal pattern): Works for every operator archetype — sales-driven or data-driven, single-truck or multi-location, new or experienced. Every operator has repeat bookings and wonders if they're still worth returning to. Replaces an earlier "event selection" framing that was too Wok-O-specific.
Revenue timing block (money-focused operational positive): Balances the negative weather anchor with a positive gain-framed outcome. Specific dollar amount ("operators captured $X more per week") makes the catalyst claim concrete. Week-scale aggregation matches how operators actually think about cash flow — bigger, more meaningful numbers than per-event deltas.
Event-to-operator direct positioning block: Category-level differentiator. Every other mobile-vendor platform either takes commission, mediates between organizer and operator, or positions itself as a marketplace where operators compete on price. VendCast explicitly doesn't. "No middleman, no commission" is a pain point every operator recognizes from prior platform experiences. Acts as live because the capability ships before public launch.
Keep hero area open: Phase 6 commit deliberately left the hero empty as reserved space for Brad's future design contribution. Respecting that reservation keeps his creative authority intact rather than filling his canvas with Claude-generated content.
Tighten feature grid copy: Current live copy has editorial em-dash style ("Connect Square, Clover, Toast, or SumUp — sales log themselves. Or import a spreadsheet.") that's cleverer than it is immediately clear. Tightening to 1-2 sentences max respects reader time and improves scannability.
Keep stats row as-is: 1,985+ events / 4 out of 5 forecasts land in range / 5 years of operator history is strong existing credibility signal. Specific modest numbers read as honest — most SaaS overclaims accuracy. The "4 out of 5 in range" framing replaced "within 16% accuracy" 2026-05-07 because the percent-accuracy framing parses on first read as "wrong (100-X)% of the time" — exactly the wrong inference.
Simplify bottom CTA: Current dual-CTA ("Start free trial / Try the calculator") weakens both options. Single dominant path is clearer. "14 days free, no credit card required" does the objection-handling without needing to list pricing.
Orange color system as Brad's call: Orange is currently doing three incompatible jobs (brand accent in logo, chart data color, CTA background). Design systems should have one role per color. Brad owns brand decisions and has 30+ years of luxury design experience — this is exactly his territory, not Julian's to decide unilaterally.
Check second logo glyph in hero: Mockup showed what appeared to be a second VendCast logo rendering below the nav. Could be unintentional artifact or could be intentional — worth flagging either way.
Monochrome logo version consideration: The dual-color "Vend" teal + "Cast" orange wordmark works in the mockup but may fight other elements in contexts like email headers, small favicons, or overlays on busy backgrounds. Brad's call whether that's needed now or later.
Tighten hero vertical padding: If hero stays text-only (hero area reserved for Brad's fill), the current teal block may feel oversized for just a headline and subline. Visual proportion consideration.
Update subline from "food truck operator" to "mobile vendors": The TruckCast-to-VendCast rename was deliberately about broadening beyond food trucks to all mobile vendors (carts, trailers, popups, etc.). The current subline narrows the framing back to food trucks, contradicting the broader positioning. Worth updating to match actual target market.
Placeholder numbers in insight blocks: Real data will be pulled before anything goes live. Writing placeholder syntax now lets structure get built without fake numbers shipping accidentally, and gives a clean replacement pass later.
